## FAQ

### Can I use openminicrew for free?

Yes — under the AGPL-3.0 license. If you comply with its terms (primarily:
making your source code available to users who interact with your service),
you pay nothing.

### I want to run a bot for my company. Do I need a commercial license?

It depends. If you run the bot only internally (employees only, not exposed
over a public network), AGPL-3.0 may be sufficient. If external users interact
with your bot, you must either open-source your full system under AGPL-3.0 or
obtain a commercial license.

### Does the AGPL-3.0 apply to my tools and integrations?

If your code links with, calls, or depends on openminicrew in a way that makes
it a derivative work, the AGPL-3.0 applies to the combined work. External
services connected only through standard APIs (e.g., Telegram Bot API, Google
APIs) are generally not affected.

agree to license your contribution under both the AGPL-3.0 and the commercial
license, so that the project can continue to offer dual licensing. A
Contributor License Agreement (CLA) may be required for significant
contributions.

available under MIT for those who obtained it before the change. All new code
and subsequent releases are under AGPL-3.0 + Commercial dual license.
